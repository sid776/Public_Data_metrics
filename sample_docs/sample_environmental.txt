Environmental and Social Framework — Sample Excerpt

1. Environmental and social risks in project design
Projects supported by the institution must identify, assess, and manage environmental and social risks. Key risk areas include: (a) impacts on natural habitats and biodiversity, (b) pollution and resource use, (c) community health and safety, (d) displacement and livelihoods, and (e) impacts on indigenous peoples and cultural heritage.

2. Climate change considerations
All major projects are required to consider climate change mitigation and adaptation. This includes assessing greenhouse gas emissions, resilience of infrastructure to extreme weather, and alignment with host country NDCs where applicable.

3. Stakeholder engagement
Meaningful consultation with affected communities and other stakeholders is mandatory. Grievance mechanisms must be established to allow individuals and groups to raise concerns and receive a response.

4. Labor and working conditions
Contractors and sub-contractors must comply with core labor standards, including non-discrimination, freedom of association, and safe working conditions. Child labor and forced labor are prohibited.

5. Monitoring and reporting
Borrowers must monitor environmental and social performance and report on implementation of the agreed management plans. Independent third-party oversight may be required for high-risk projects.
