Social Safeguards and Inclusion — Key Provisions

Definition of social safeguards
Social safeguards are policies and procedures that ensure development projects do not harm people and that benefits are shared equitably. They cover involuntary resettlement, indigenous peoples, labor conditions, and community health and safety.

Involuntary resettlement
When projects require land acquisition or restriction of access to resources, a Resettlement Action Plan (RAP) or Resettlement Policy Framework (RPF) is required. Affected persons must be compensated at full replacement cost and supported to restore or improve their livelihoods. Preference is given to land-based resettlement when affected households depend on land.

Indigenous peoples
Projects affecting indigenous peoples require free, prior, and informed consent (FPIC) where impacts are significant. A dedicated Indigenous Peoples Plan or Planning Framework must be prepared. Cultural heritage and traditional knowledge must be respected and protected.

Gender and inclusion
Projects must promote gender equality and social inclusion. Analysis should identify differential impacts on women, youth, and marginalized groups. Beneficiary engagement and grievance mechanisms must be accessible to all.

Grievance redress
Borrowers must establish a grievance mechanism that is transparent, accessible, and responsive. Complaints should be logged, investigated, and resolved within agreed timeframes. Escalation paths must be clear.
